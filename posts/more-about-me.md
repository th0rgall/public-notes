## 🔨 The Name 

![Thorgal](./assets/more-about-me/image.png)

First things first. About my name: yes. It directly refers to [the almighty god](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thor). Indirectly, it is an apt wordplay from my dad on one of his favorite comic book series: [Thorgal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thorgal) (Thor Galle, Thorgal, get it?). I hold no grudge, it's a great series.

## 🇧🇪 The Formative Years

Thorgal is also a Belgian series, which brings me to the start of my story: I lived most of my 20-something years in [Leuven, Belgium](https://www.openstreetmap.org/relation/196311). I grew up there happily, went to a fun [Freinet](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/C%C3%A9lestin_Freinet) primary school (no homework!), a proper Catholic high school (homework!), and finally, a [world-renowned university](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/KU_Leuven) for my bachelors' in Computer Science.

![Naamsestraat - 20190418_110225.jpg](./assets/more-about-me/20190418_110225.jpg)

All that in the same cozy medieval university town. Thus, when graduating from my bachelor's in 2018, I felt the urge to expand my horizons.

## 🇫🇮🇸🇪 The Nordic Years

I left my dear Belgium, 2500 km [by bike](https://www.instagram.com/explore/tags/thepedalchoiceog/), to pursue an European master's program in Human-Computer Interaction & Design, [at EIT Digital](https://masterschool.eitdigital.eu/human-computer-interaction-and-design).

My studies brought me to [Aalto University](https://www.aalto.fi/en) near Helsinki (Finland) for a year, and [KTH Royal Institute of Technology](https://www.kth.se/en/) in Stockholm (Sweden) for another.

![Helsinki from plane - 20190417_201602.jpg](./assets/more-about-me/20190417_201602.jpg)

In the summer of 2019 I interned at [Columbia Road](https://columbiaroad.com) in Helsinki, a digital sales consultancy. I liked the job and stayed on, taking the job with me to Stockholm, where they had just opened a new office. I lived and worked in Stockholm for two years (more [about that work here](/work#Columbia-Road)). 

In the summer of 2021, I moved back to Helsinki for reasons of ❤️. This coincided with me becoming the Product Lead of reallyreadit, inc., the company behind [Readup](/projects/readup) - a role I held for half a year, until funding ran out. Readup was a labor of love, which is why the platform still exists today, maintained by its community (including me). Afterwards, I worked for half a year as a freelance developer and designer via my own firm BitHarmony.

In the autumn of 2022, I became the Chief Technologist of Slowby, the Belgian social enterprise behind [Welcome To My Garden](/projects/welcome-to-my-garden). While still living in Helsinki and working remotely most of the time, this has brought me back closer to Belgium. In these fast-paced times, it's incredibly rewarding to help thousands of slow travellers and hosts connect with each other, and to build tools to help them plan their journeys.

## 🧶 Hobbies

I like to read regularly, which I often do online [on Readup](https://readup.org/@thorgalle).

I find **languages** fascinating. I'm currently fluent in Dutch (native) and English. I'm also able to hold a decent conversation in Swedish and French, though I'm not getting much practice as of late. I enjoy hacking on language-learning technology, so I was lucky to be able to do my master's thesis on this topic, at [Språkkraft](/work/#Sprakkraft).

In general, I like scratching my own technological itches, and doing fun stuff with my personal data (see [my projects](/projects#projects)). I also think we could collectively do much more interesting things with our personal data. In 2020 I gave [a talk about getting your personal data from big tech & doing something with it](https://2020.openbelgium.be/blogpost/big-tech-has-your-data-come-get-it). This ties in with an interest in productivity apps and knowledge management tools. My personal [Notion](https://www.notion.so/) has become massive since I started it in 2019. I'm looking for ways to publish some notes here in long-lasting, self-owned Markdown.

I enjoy being active, but as someone who also enjoys sitting in front of a computer; there's always the goal of being *more* active.

🧗🏼‍♂️ In my teens, **wall climbing** and bouldering represented a considerable part of my week. Since higher education, I've had on & off relationship with the sport. The practice never turned super serious; so I'm stuck at a ~6c grade. Bouldering can be an very fun problem solving, away from the computer.

🏃🏼‍♂️ I'm **running regularly** for all the good reasons. No races yet, though my first half marathon seems to be approaching. Also, [I'm in no hurry here](https://readup.org/comments/wired/to-run-my-best-marathon-at-age-44-i-had-to-outrun-my-past).

I've also done my fair share of tour cycling, the most intrepid one being a one-month, 2500km trip that took me from Leuven to Helsinki in 2018. A different beast entirely, but a glorious one.
